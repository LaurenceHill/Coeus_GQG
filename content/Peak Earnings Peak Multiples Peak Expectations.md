# Peak Earnings, Peak Multiples, Peak Expectations

**Source:** A Change of Pace | CIO Perspectives (Apr 2024)
**Tags:** #valuation #bubble #2021 #tech #mental-model

---

## Summary

GQG's three-factor framework for diagnosing a market top. When earnings, multiples, and expectations all peak simultaneously, the downside is severe because there is no margin of safety across any dimension. They used this framework to exit tech in 2021 and to distinguish the 2024 tech rally from that earlier bubble.

---

## Explanation

In early 2021, GQG concluded that technology stocks had hit a simultaneous triple peak:

- **Peak Earnings**: Near-term earnings were at cyclically elevated levels — unsustainably high
- **Peak Multiples**: Valuations (P/E, EV/Sales etc.) were at or near historic highs — no room for multiple expansion
- **Peak Expectations**: Consensus growth forecasts were maximally optimistic — the market had priced in perfection

When all three peaks align, even a small disappointment on any dimension triggers a significant de-rating. "Profitable and unprofitable tech continued to surge regardless of fundamentals — until the music stopped."

---

## 2024 Was Different From 2021 (GQG's View at the Time)

In April 2024, GQG argued the tech rally had a materially different character:

- **NVIDIA** in 2021 traded at ~65x forward earnings with growth that didn't justify it. By 2024, NVIDIA had re-rated _downward_ (to ~30x forward) while actual EPS surged — meaning it got _cheaper_ despite the price rise
- **Walmart** contrast: a steady-eddy business at 17x forward earnings is attractive; at 23x it isn't — illustrating that price paid relative to growth rate matters enormously
- 2024 tech was supported by real, fundamental earnings growth — not narrative alone

**Key principle:** Willingness to pay 30x for a company growing 30%+ is rational; paying 23x for a company growing in high single digits is not.

---

## ⚠️ Contradiction with Later View

_Against the Grain_ (Jan 2026) reveals that GQG subsequently **cut AI/tech exposure significantly in 2025**, citing precisely the same bubble dynamics they said were _absent_ in 2024. This is not necessarily a contradiction — it shows the bubble diagnosis arrived later — but it raises the question of timing:

- Apr 2024: Tech is fundamentals-driven, unlike 2021 — GQG overweight
- 2025: GQG cuts AI exposure, citing unsustainable valuations and narrative-driven mania
- Cost: "significant relative performance" underperformance in 2025

This sequence illustrates the difficulty of [[Bubble Timing — Early vs Late]] even for disciplined fundamental managers.
