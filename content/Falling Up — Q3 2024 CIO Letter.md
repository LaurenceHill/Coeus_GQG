# Falling Up — Q3 2024 CIO Letter

**Source:** Falling Up | CIO Perspectives (Oct 2024)
**Tags:** #CIO-letter #Q3-2024 #China #India #GLP-1 #semiconductors #consumer-staples #utilities #relative-performance

---

## Summary

Q3 2024 letter — titled "The paradox of progress." GQG's relative performance across all core strategies lagged benchmarks for the quarter, primarily due to two factors: underweight China (which rallied sharply in September 2024) and underperformance of prior-year winners (GLP-1 stocks, AI beneficiaries, India positions). GQG held firm on all major positions, initiated a rotation from technology toward consumer staples and utilities, and restated their scepticism on China's sustainability.

---

## What Went Wrong in Q3 2024

Three contributors to underperformance:
1. **China September rally:** MSCI China posted a sharp double-digit monthly return. GQG was underweight China — their core positioning given structural concerns about governance, geopolitics, and earnings quality.
2. **GLP-1 stocks (Eli Lilly, Novo Nordisk):** Core holdings that had been major positive contributors over the prior year gave back some performance.
3. **AI beneficiaries and India names:** Similarly, prior-year winners experienced rotational underperformance.

GQG's response: nothing fundamental changed. "Very little has changed for the outlook of those companies." They maintained all positions.

**Longer-term context:** Despite Q3 2024 underperformance, all core strategies remained positive in absolute terms and outperformed their benchmarks over 1-, 3-, 5-, and 10-year periods and since inception. The quarter was a short-term reversal within a longer track record of outperformance.

---

## China: The "Sugar High" Assessment

GQG's verdict on the September 2024 China rally: unsustainable.

Historical data (since Xi Jinping assumed power in March 2013):
- **8 double-digit monthly returns** for MSCI China (vs. only 2 for the S&P 500)
- **In 6 of those 8 episodes**, MSCI China returns were **negative one year after the rally** — approximately -10% on average

GQG framed the September 2024 rally as another "sugar high" — a pattern of policy-driven sentiment spikes that historically dissipate. This directly extends [[China Equity Market Skepticism]].

**Within China, GQG's preference was for "old economy" names:** state-owned enterprises in banking and oil/gas, rather than "new economy" names in the regulatory crosshairs. If the economy meaningfully strengthened, they expected cyclicals to outperform — but remained unconvinced this was likely.

---

## India vs. China: The EPS Growth Divergence

GQG used this letter to reinforce the India vs. China earnings comparison:

> *"We would rather go where the fundamentals continue to improve and are likely to remain durable rather than rely on a short pop in sentiment."*

India (NIFTY 50) forward EPS growth has materially outpaced China (CSI 300) since 2010. GQG's preference: India offers **faster and more dependable growth** without China's regulatory headwinds. The India transformation, in their view, was only likely to improve over coming years.

This is consistent with [[India Investment Case and Adani]] and [[Emerging Markets vs Developed Markets]].

---

## The Rotation: Away from Semiconductors, Toward Defensives

Q3 2024 saw GQG continue rotating from technology toward consumer staples and utilities. The valuation-based reasoning:

| Sector | Relative P/S vs MSCI USA Equal Weighted | GQG View |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer Staples | Near 10-year median | Relatively attractive |
| Utilities | Near 10-year median | Relatively attractive |
| SOX (Semiconductors) | **30%+ above 10-year median** | Requires greater vigilance; trimmed on weakness |

> *"Valuations are like hiking a mountain: the higher the altitude, the less oxygen we have, and the more cautious we need to be."*

GQG trimmed tech "more aggressively" as any slowdown signal warrants faster reaction times — a direct expression of [[Peak Earnings Peak Multiples Peak Expectations]] framework.

This rotation (begun in Q3 2024) is the embryo of the full defensive thesis articulated in *Is Defensive the New Offensive?* (Feb 2026) and *Against the Grain* (Jan 2026). The direction was clear by October 2024, well before the AI bubble thesis was formally published.

---

## GLP-1 Conviction Maintained

Despite underperformance, GQG doubled down on Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk:

> *"Novo and Lilly are yet to become obese."*

Meaning: the obesity treatment market is still in very early penetration. GLP-1 adoption remained far from peak. The Q4 2024 Novo Nordisk selloff (detailed in [[India Investment Case and Adani]]) was later described by GQG as the moment they increased the position.

---

## ⚠️ Note: China Rally Was Temporary

The September 2024 China rally reversed as GQG predicted. [[China Equity Market Skepticism]] and [[China Property Crisis and Macro Deterioration]] (Oct 2025 source: *Clouded Optimism in China's Markets*) confirm GQG's subsequent analysis — by late 2025 the pattern held: returns were negative roughly one year after the September 2024 spike.
