# GQG Investment Philosophy

**Sources:** A Change of Pace (CIO, Apr 2024); Against the Grain (CIO, Jan 2026); 2022 Capital Gains Tax-Alanche Risk (Nov 2022)
**Tags:** #philosophy #quality-growth #process #rajiv-jain

---

## Summary

GQG Partners is a quality-first, fundamentals-driven global equity manager. Their edge is asymmetric: strong downside protection in bear markets, accepting of underperformance in frothy, narrative-driven markets. Their approach is adaptive rather than dogmatic — they will revise positions when data changes — but they will not abandon fundamental analysis for hype-driven investing.

---

## Core Tenets

**1. Quality & Durability First**
GQG seeks businesses with sustainable earnings, strong fundamentals, and durable competitive advantages. They will pay a premium for quality but are not indifferent to price.

**2. Downside Protection Over FOMO**

> _"Our best performance historically has generally come in bear markets and our worst performance has come in frothy markets."_
> — Against the Grain, Jan 2026

Over full market cycles, limiting downside — even at the cost of upside participation — has produced meaningful outperformance in net terms. This is a deliberate structural bias.

**3. Munger's "Not Stupid" Principle**

> _"It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."_
> — Charlie Munger, cited in A Change of Pace (Apr 2024), sourced from Wesco Annual Report, 1989

GQG explicitly cites this as guiding their approach — prioritizing the avoidance of folly over the pursuit of brilliance. Perfectly aligned with Charlie Munger's inversion framework.

**4. Adaptive, Not Dogmatic**

- GQG will stay still when things are working ("resist the urge to move things around")
- But they will react when data changes — they revisited their AI thesis "numerous times" before holding firm
- They deploy "non-traditional analysts in the field" as part of their diligence process

**5. Micro Over Macro**
Conviction is driven by company-level fundamentals, not macroeconomic forecasting. The 2024 letter explicitly notes that "conviction to stay the course was driven by micro, rather than macro."

**6. Fiduciary Discipline**

> _"We cannot break our fiduciary duty with our clients and invest in hype when the math does not make sense to us."_
> — Against the Grain, Jan 2026

## Performance Pattern

| Market Condition | GQG Relative Performance |
|---|---|
| Bear markets / corrections | Historically strong |
| Frothy / narrative-driven markets | Historically weak |
| Full cycle | Meaningful outperformance |

This pattern held in 2025 when their AI underweight cost them significant relative performance in a rising market.
