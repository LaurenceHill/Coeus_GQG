# AI Data Center Debt Bubble

**Source:** Dotcom on Steroids: Part IV | GQG Research (Mar 2026)
**Tags:** #AI #data-centers #debt #CoreWeave #neocloud #bubble #leverage

---

## Summary

By March 2026, the AI infrastructure buildout had generated a fragile, debt-fueled financial architecture concentrated in smaller "neocloud" developers. Data center debt issuance surged to $183B in 2025 (up from $92B the prior year). CoreWeave — with \$21.4B in debt against revenue barely able to cover interest — represents GQG's clearest example of what they call "the worst balance sheet" in US tech. GQG's thesis: when this cycle turns, the fractures will appear first not in the hyperscalers' financials, but in the builders predicated on everything going perfectly.

---

## The Debt Iceberg

Data center debt issuance surged from $92B (2024) to $183B (2025), per S\&P Global. Hyperscalers and cloud service providers drove most of this via corporate bond markets, but off-balance-sheet transactions have also surfaced — historically a late-cycle signal.

Key datapoints:

- Morgan Stanley projects a **\$1.5T financing gap** for global data center construction and AI infrastructure through 2028 — over half expected to be filled by private credit
- Oracle: total debt surged 40% year-over-year to \$124B; CDS spreads tripled their five-year average
- Meta financed a \$27B data center in Louisiana through a **special purpose vehicle (SPV)** with higher interest rates, masking leverage from its own balance sheet
- Alphabet successfully raised \$30B+ in under 24 hours across multiple currencies (one of the fastest corporate bond sales in history) — but signs of stress elsewhere are intensifying

## The Neocloud Fragility Thesis

While hyperscalers bear headline debt, GQG argues the true risk lies in **neoclouds** — smaller non-hyperscaler data center builders:

- Non-hyperscaler firms are responsible for **64% of the 647 data centers** slated for completion by end of 2027 (per S\&P Global 451 Research)
- Six key neocloud players have amassed over **\$32B in debt** in just a few years, with little credit history, as VC fundraising remains near decade lows
- Business models hinge on demand from a handful of clients (Anthropic, Meta, OpenAI) — a highly concentrated customer base
- Examples:
  - **Nebius Group** (Amsterdam): debt more than tripled in two years to \$4.6B
  - **Applied Digital Corporation** (Dallas): debt grew from $36M to $700M in two years

The structural vulnerability: data centers require **front-loaded capital** (land, electrical equipment, power commitments) while revenue is **backloaded** and contingent on tenant ramp-up and pricing. Any single delay, rate reset, or demand wobble can trigger covenant pressure or project cancellation.

**Fermi example:** Amazon terminated a \$150M advance commitment for Fermi's West Texas AI campus; the stock dropped over a third. A class-action lawsuit followed, alleging Fermi misled the market about tenant demand.

## CoreWeave: "The Worst Balance Sheet"

CoreWeave is GQG's primary case study for debt-fueled AI infrastructure fragility:

| Metric | Detail |
|---|---|
| GPUs | 300,000+ advanced Nvidia GPUs across 43 data centers |
| Debt (end-2025) | $21.4B at interest rates exceeding 10% |
| 2025 Total Revenue | $5.1B |
| 2025 Interest + Principal Payments | ~$4.6B (nearly all revenue consumed) |
| Q4 2025 Net Loss | $452M (interest payments of $388M in that quarter alone) |
| IPO (2026) | Raised $1.5B vs $2.7B target |
| Backlog | $67B in committed agreements (OpenAI, Meta, Microsoft) |

> _"CoreWeave has one of the worst balance sheets I have ever seen—and I mean it."_ — Gil Luria, Head of Technology Research, D.A. Davidson

### GPU-Collateralized Loans (DDTL Facilities)

CoreWeave's primary funding mechanism is **Delayed Draw Term Loan (DDTL)** facilities, asset-backed and collateralized by GPUs:

- **DDTL 1.0** (Blackstone/Magnetar): $2.3B at **15% interest rate** — costs ~$250M/quarter to service through 2028
- **DDTL 2.0**: terms between DDTL 1 and 3
- **DDTL 3.0** (Morgan Stanley/MUFG/Goldman Sachs): $2.6B at 9%; creditors required $100M restricted cash (double the \$50M required for DDTL 2.0 — a sign of increasing caution)
- **Total DDTL**: \$15.5B across four facilities, ~60% already drawn

**Collateral mismatch:** CoreWeave depreciates GPUs over 6 years; GQG's proprietary research puts the real economic life at 2–3 years (consistent with their [[Hyperscaler CapEx Circular Accounting]] analysis). If GPU pricing falls or refresh cycles accelerate, collateral coverage and cash flows erode quickly.

Third-party tracker Silicon Data has already documented meaningful declines in rental prices for Nvidia's advanced B200 GPUs — confirming the collateral erosion risk is not theoretical.

## Systemic Risk Architecture

GQG's argument is structural: **the boom's most fragile leverage is concentrated in the least resilient part of the ecosystem**. Hyperscaler stress will show in CDS spreads and off-balance-sheet vehicles; neocloud stress will show in covenant breaches, project cancellations, and defaults.

Blue Owl Capital (managing \$305B in assets), a major data center financier, was reportedly grappling with liquidity challenges as retail-facing non-traded vehicles faced redemptions during tech sector selloffs — creating a feedback loop between private credit markets and AI infrastructure financing.
