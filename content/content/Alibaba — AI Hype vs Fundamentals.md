# Alibaba — AI Hype vs Fundamentals

**Source:** Clouded Optimism in China's Markets | GQG Research (Oct 2025)
**Tags:** #alibaba #china #AI #ecommerce #valuation #bubble #fundamentals

---

## Summary

GQG's October 2025 analysis used Alibaba as a case study in how narrative-driven markets can sustain large valuation premiums despite deteriorating fundamentals. With shares up 70%+ YTD on AI excitement, Alibaba's underlying business showed slowing e-commerce earnings, intensifying competition, negative free cash flow, and a $53B AI spending plan into a market generating only $1.5B in annual AI application revenue — the same capital misallocation pattern GQG identified more broadly in the [[AI Bubble 2025–2026]].

---

## The Setup

Alibaba's stock rallied 70%+ YTD in 2025, driven by:

- DeepSeek's success (early 2025) sparking AI optimism about Chinese tech
- Progress in Alibaba's internal chip design (T-Head) and cloud AI services
- General market enthusiasm for China's AI build-out

GQG's view: the rally was built on "speculative optimism" disconnected from financial reality.

---

## Fundamental Deterioration

**Revenue (Q1 FY2026, ending June 2025):**

- Total revenue: CNY247.7B (\$34.8B) — up only **2% YoY**, missing estimates of CNY252.9B
- Growth driven by Alibaba Cloud (+26%) — but Cloud is only ~13% of revenue
- "All Others" segment (Cainiao logistics, Amap navigation, ~24% of revenue): **-28% YoY**
- Core China e-commerce (Taobao/Tmall, ~57% of revenue): up 10% in revenue, but **adjusted EBIT -21% YoY** due to heavy instant-commerce investment

**Profitability collapse:**

- Operating profit: **-3% YoY**
- Non-GAAP net income: fell significantly (headline net income boosted by equity investment gains and sale of Trendyol unit — non-recurring)
- EBITA margins deteriorating trend 2021→2025

**Cash flow disaster:**

- Operating cash flow: CNY20.7B, **-39% YoY**
- Free cash flow: **-CNY18.8B outflow** vs. +CNY17B inflow a year ago — a ~CNY36B swing

---

## The Competition Problem

Alibaba is fighting a multi-front war:

- **PDD (Pinduoduo)**: low-price e-commerce challenger
- **Douyin (TikTok China)**: social commerce
- **JD.com + Meituan**: now entering instant commerce, forcing Alibaba to commit CNY50B (~\$7B) in subsidies over 12 months just to defend market share
- China's regulator (SAMR) simultaneously demanding platforms resist "vicious subsidies" — putting Alibaba in an impossible bind: subsidise to compete or cede ground

Jack Ma re-emerged in 2025 from years of post-regulatory-crackdown seclusion, now reportedly shaping AI strategy — a positive optics move, but raising questions about governance continuity.

---

## The AI Spending Trap

Alibaba committed **CNY380B (\$53B) over 3 years** on AI and cloud infrastructure, with capex surging **220% YoY** to CNY39B in a single quarter.

The problem: China's **entire AI application annual recurring revenue = ~\$1.5B** (Goldman Sachs, Aug 2025).

GQG's question: _"How long will these internet giants continue pouring billions into acquiring GPUs and building infrastructure"_ when the revenue base for AI applications is a fraction of the investment?

Unlike US peers (Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud) who can monetise AI infrastructure across vast global enterprise customers, Alibaba faces:

- US export controls forcing expensive in-house chip development (T-Head) instead of using NVIDIA H100s
- A commoditised token/cloud market with "persistently low prices"
- No "game-changing AI applications on the horizon"

**Debt funding the gap:** Debt-to-equity ratio rising as Alibaba issues bonds to fund what free cash flow can no longer cover.

## Analytical Framework

GQG applied the same [[Multiple Compression Risk]] and [[Peak Earnings Peak Multiples Peak Expectations]] lens to Alibaba that it applied to US AI stocks and spirits. The market was pricing in AI transformation; the fundamentals showed a mature e-commerce business with eroding margins funding a speculative AI bet into a tiny revenue pool.
