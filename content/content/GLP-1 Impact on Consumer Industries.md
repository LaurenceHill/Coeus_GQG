# GLP-1 Impact on Consumer Industries

**Source:** A Spirited Debate | GQG Research (May 2024); Against the Grain | CIO Perspectives (Jan 2026)
**Tags:** #GLP-1 #semaglutide #consumer #obesity #structural-risk

---

## Summary

GLP-1 receptor agonists (Semaglutide/Tirzepatide — Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) are a structural risk to multiple consumer industries beyond their primary use in diabetes and obesity. GQG flagged alcohol as particularly exposed. By Jan 2026, GLP-1 appears in GQG's thinking as a persistent background risk for food and beverage companies.

---

## Explanation

### What GLP-1 Drugs Do

GLP-1 receptor agonists reduce appetite and modulate reward pathways. Originally developed for type 2 diabetes and approved for obesity, they also appear to reduce cravings for alcohol and potentially other addictive substances.

### The Alcohol Channel

From a _Nature_ study (Nov 2023): real-world evidence of reduced alcohol consumption in obese individuals taking semaglutide or tirzepatide. Animal studies showed reduced alcohol intake; this extended to initial human data.

The mechanism matters: GLP-1 drugs suppress the dopamine reward response associated with alcohol. This is not merely calorie-counting — it alters the craving itself.

**Concentration risk amplifier:** Given that the top 10% of US drinkers account for ~50% of all alcohol consumed ([[Alcohol Industry Structural Decline]]), GLP-1 use among heavy drinkers could have outsized industry impact disproportionate to overall drug adoption rates.

### GQG's Assessment (May 2024)

"Early innings" — not yet visible in volume numbers, but a credible forward risk that the market appeared to be underpricing, particularly for spirits companies trading at near-market multiples.

### By January 2026

In _Against the Grain_, GQG mentions Heineken's attractiveness despite GLP-1 risks, adding the qualifier: _"Heineken has an expansive emerging markets footprint as beer consumption continues to grow globally — with or without GLP-1s."_ This suggests:

1. GLP-1 risk is now a standard factor in GQG's consumer analysis
2. Emerging market exposure can partially offset developed market GLP-1 headwinds (drug adoption is predominantly a developed market phenomenon currently)

## Industries with Potential GLP-1 Exposure

| Industry | Mechanism | GQG View |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol | Reduced cravings, especially heavy drinkers | Flagged as structural risk (2024) |
| Ultra-processed food | Reduced appetite/cravings | Implied risk; not explicitly analyzed in batch 1 |
| Tobacco | Potential craving suppression | Not explicitly addressed |
| Obesity / Weight loss (Lilly, Novo) | Primary indication; treatment at scale | **Long position**: GQG held Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk as core holdings; see [[India Investment Case and Adani]] for Eli Lilly conviction detail |
| Healthcare (devices/pharma) | Reduced obesity comorbidities | Net negative for some; positive for GLP-1 manufacturers |
