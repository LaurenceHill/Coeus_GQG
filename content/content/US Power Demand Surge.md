# US Power Demand Surge

**Source:** Charging Ahead | GQG Research (Sep 2024)
**Tags:** #power #utilities #AI #data-centers #EVs #grid #energy-transition

---

## Summary

After two decades of flat electricity demand, the US is entering a structural era of accelerating power consumption driven by three forces: AI/data center expansion, EV adoption, and the broader electrification of the economy. GQG's September 2024 paper framed utilities and power infrastructure as a way to gain AI exposure beyond semiconductors and software, with demand expected to grow at least 2% per year for the next two decades.

---

## Why Demand Was Flat for 20 Years

US electricity demand grew at ~5% annually in the second half of the 20th century, then flatlined from ~2000 onward. Reasons:

- Dramatic improvements in energy efficiency (LED lighting, efficient appliances, industrial equipment)
- Offshoring of heavy industrial operations to developing countries (steel, manufacturing)

---

## The Three New Demand Drivers

### 1. AI / Hyperscale Data Centers

A new generation of AI-optimised data centres consumes **4-5x more power** than traditional data centres (historically concentrated in Loudoun County, Virginia). The most recent surge:

- Expected to grow at **10% per year through 2030**
- Could triple from 2.5% of total US electricity consumption (2022) to 7.5% by the end of the decade
- Equivalent to electricity use of ~40 million US homes

Geographic shift: Data centres are moving away from the I-66 corridor near Washington DC to California, Ohio, Illinois, Georgia, Florida, and other regions — requiring grid expansion into new areas.

PJM, MISO, CAISO, and Southeast regions expected to hold >60% of total US data centre capacity by 2027.

**GQG framing:** _"Investing in the power and utility sectors is a way to play the AI boom beyond semiconductors and software companies."_

### 2. EV Adoption

Every EV that replaces an internal combustion engine vehicle adds **80 kWh** of annual electricity demand. At current trajectory:

- Total annual EV load could rise from ~24,000 GWh (2023) to **468,000 GWh by 2040** — a 1,850% increase
- Equivalent to total electricity generated annually in Texas
- Could represent 9-12% of projected US grid capacity by 2040

EV charging creates a distinctive grid challenge: ultra-fast chargers draw power equivalent to a "super Walmart" — but unlike a real Walmart, they pop up randomly across the grid at unpredictable times. A former regulator described the transmission, interconnectedness, and substation investment required as "mind-boggling."

### 3. Fuel Switching to Clean Energy

- Renewables already generate >20% of all US electricity; expected to more than double in 15 years
- The switch to renewables requires **3x the generation capacity** due to intermittency (solar and wind are unreliable on peak demand days)
- Coal use declining in absolute terms, but still expected to be 73% of coal sector consumption
- Nuclear: generating ~20% of US electricity since 1990; Plant Vogtle Units 3 and 4 came online in 2023-2024 — the first new US nuclear reactors since 1996; no additional plants planned

**Intermittency risk**: Texas Christmas 2022 — windmills froze, solar panels under snow, 40,000 MW of renewables rendered useless in a crisis

---

## Grid Infrastructure Deficit

Utilities and grid operators described as "already way behind" in planning for these demand levels. PJM (the northeastern US grid operator) was specifically called out as under-prepared. The pace of demand growth is outrunning the pace at which new power facilities can be permitted, financed, and built.

---

## ⚠️ Temporal Tension with Later GQG View

_Against the Grain_ (Jan 2026) explicitly states GQG "stayed away from utility investments that were predicated on the AI boom for base load growth." This appears to partially qualify the _Charging Ahead_ thesis (Sep 2024), which framed utilities as an AI proxy. The evolution:

- Sep 2024: Utilities = way to play AI boom in power infrastructure
- Jan 2026: Cautious on utilities whose thesis depends on AI demand materialising; favour utilities with structural demand independent of AI (e.g., American Water Works)

**Interpretation:** Not a full contradiction — GQG's 2026 utility position is selective (water utilities, non-AI-dependent power demand) rather than a blanket rejection. But the AI-powered data centre thesis for utilities was somewhat walked back as GQG became more sceptical of the AI narrative generally ([[AI Bubble 2025–2026]]).
