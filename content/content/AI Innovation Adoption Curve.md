# AI Innovation Adoption Curve

**Source:** Decoding the Impending AI Boom | GQG Research (Jul 2024)
**Tags:** #AI #adoption #innovation #semiconductors #infrastructure #NVIDIA #technology

---

## Summary

GQG's July 2024 paper framed AI as being in the "early adopter" stage of Everett Rogers's Innovation-Adoption Curve — with meaningful productivity impact still 3–5 years away for most enterprises. The near-term investment opportunity was concentrated in foundational infrastructure: chips, semiconductor equipment, and data centres. This paper represents GQG's most constructive moment on AI — written five months after their April 2024 bullish stance and approximately 18 months before their AI bubble declaration in January 2026.

---

## Rogers's Innovation-Adoption Curve Applied to AI

The five stages of Rogers's model (1962):

1. **Innovators** — earliest experimenters
2. **Early Adopters** — current stage as of Jul 2024 (GQG's assessment)
3. **Early Majority** — where enterprises will measure ROI rigorously before committing
4. **Late Majority** — out-of-the-box solutions emerge; fundamentally changes business practices
5. **Laggards** — mass adoption

**GQG's Jul 2024 positioning:** _"We believe we are still at the nascent stage of 'early adopters.'"_ Evidence: LLM applications like ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot are live but productivity gains are not yet systematically measured or confirmed enterprise-wide.

More than half of CEOs in a KPMG survey expected AI profits **3–5 years out** — consistent with the gap between early-adopter and early-majority stages.

## Why This AI Cycle Is Different

GQG identified three factors that make AI's trajectory more durable than prior technology hypes (Metaverse, etc.):

1. **Data deluge**: The explosion of digital data provides the raw training material that prior generations of AI lacked. The diversity and volume of data generated by the internet is an unprecedented resource.

2. **Computing power**: Moore's Law finally caught up with AI's demands. Transistor density enables the parallel processing that large language models require.

3. **Capital**: Unlike past AI booms with cash-constrained startups, today's AI leaders (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta) have massive cash-flowing businesses funding capex at unprecedented scale. Combined, the largest six cloud companies spent **$160B on capex in 2023** (~0.6% of US GDP). Jensen Huang projected global spending on datacenter infrastructure could reach **$2 trillion** within 4–5 years.

**Unanimous support:** AI is rare among emerging technologies in having cross-industry, cross-competitor consensus. Even normally fierce rivals formed alliances (Partnership on AI). Contrasted with the Metaverse — which faced divided responses — AI has near-universal institutional endorsement.

## Where Investment Was Flowing (Jul 2024)

**Foundational layer (primary beneficiary):**

- High-performance chips (NVIDIA cited explicitly — purchased at ~30x forward, "virtually no multiple expansion" since purchase)
- Semiconductor equipment (EUV lithography, wafer fab equipment — WFE)
- Data centres (hyperscalers + tier-2 operators)
- Cooling systems, networking equipment, power infrastructure

**Software layer (more uneven):**
GQG was already noting in Q2 2024 (_Context is Key_) that software companies were showing earnings misses and disappointing ROI on AI integration — while NVIDIA's earnings remained "robust." This caused GQG to reduce software exposure and consolidate into NVIDIA and healthcare (Eli Lilly/GLP-1) names.

**Hugging Face data point:** AI model count on Hugging Face up **352% YoY** to ~500,000 models as of early 2024 — illustrating the breadth of AI application development.

## Historical Precedent: Underestimation Is the Norm

GQG drew on history to argue against dismissing AI's transformative potential:

- The internet was initially dismissed as a fad
- The iPhone faced widespread resistance before redefining mobile computing
- Not all technologies succeed, but _underestimation_ at early stages is the most common mistake

> _"The key takeaway is that underestimating the transformative power of a technology in its early stages is a recurring theme in history, often proven wrong by the tide of progress."_

## ⚠️ Temporal Tension: Most Constructive Moment Before the Pivot

This paper (Jul 2024) represents GQG at its **most bullish on AI** — before the bubble conviction solidified. Timeline:

- Apr 2024 (_A Change of Pace_): Overweight tech/AI; 2024 is not 2021
- Jul 2024 (_Decoding AI Boom_): AI in early-adopter phase; infrastructure investable; patient on software
- Jul 2024 (_Context is Key_): Trimming software, holding NVIDIA; "calibrated optimism"
- 2025: GQG cuts AI/tech exposure as narrative-driven mania overtakes fundamentals
- Jan 2026 (_Against the Grain_): Full bubble declaration; massive relative performance cost

The Adoption Curve paper itself acknowledged the risk of hype — GQG said they would "adapt positioning as we get more visibility on who the likely winners and losers will be." They did. But the adaptation — exiting before the bubble peaked — cost them 2025 relative returns.

This is not a contradiction but an evolution. The [[Bubble Timing — Early vs Late]] page captures the trade-off GQG consciously accepted.
