## Summary

Q1 2026 brought the closest analogue to Q1 2022 that GQG had seen: tech stocks sharply underperforming, energy sharply outperforming, and a supply-driven commodity shock replacing the fear-driven one of 2022. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz in early March 2026 removed ~20% of global seaborne crude and 25% of LNG trade in a single event. GQG's view: 2026 could be _worse_ than 2022 because this time the shock is real, not anticipated, and the AI/tech excesses accumulated since 2022 are far larger.

---

## The Strait of Hormuz Closure (March 2026)

The single most consequential event in the paper:

- Closed in early March 2026 amid Middle East conflict (Iran-related)
- Removed ~**20% of global seaborne crude oil** supply
- Removed ~**25% of global LNG trade**
- Qatar estimates: **17% of LNG production capacity lost for 3–5 years** (per Qatar Energy CEO)

Policy responses globally:

- Australia: fuel rationing
- Korea: price controls
- Europe: various gas tax exemptions
- US: relaxation of Jones Act (normally restricts domestic shipping routes)

## 2022 vs 2026: The Parallel and the Difference

**Sector performance:**
Q1 2022 and Q1 2026 S\&P 500 sector returns were "strikingly similar":

- Energy, Materials, Utilities, Consumer Staples: outperform
- IT, Consumer Discretionary, Communication Services: underperform

**The critical difference:**

- 2022: Fear of an energy shock (Russia/Ukraine). The market priced the _risk_
- 2026: An actual energy shock is being delivered. The market must price the _reality_

GQG: _"We believe the current crisis has the potential to be far worse, particularly because 2022 was driven by the fear of an energy shock, whereas 2026 is actually delivering one."_

## Capital Cycles: Underinvestment vs Overinvestment

GQG drew a sharp capital cycle contrast:

- **Energy**: Years of underinvestment (post-2014 oil crash, ESG pressure, net-zero mandates) → structurally higher returns on invested capital when supply is needed
- **Technology/AI**: Years of massive overinvestment → the opposite

> _"Many market participants may be mistaking the real world for the metaverse, where things can be built or demolished without any real consequences. Ironically, the metaverse is basically a failed $80B project... Imagine the positive impact $80B could have if it were invested in something physically useful!"_

## Tech Fundamentals: Cracks Accelerating by Q1 2026

The energy shock arrived simultaneously with independent deterioration in AI/tech fundamentals:

**Declining FCF yields for hyperscalers:** Free cash flow yields for major hyperscalers turning negative — trend line shows clear downward trajectory across 2016–2026.

**Oracle's CDS surpassing GFC peak:** Oracle's five-year credit default swap spread exceeded its Global Financial Crisis peak — market pricing credit risk into one of the main debt-funded AI infrastructure builders.

**Earnings revision concentration:** ~80% of S\&P 500 earnings revisions in 2026/27 estimates driven by a handful of AI companies. GQG: this mirrors the last innings of the 2007 banking bubble where positive bank earnings revisions continued until very late.

**Memory market rollover:** DDR5 module prices falling sharply (32GB 6400MHz DDR5: from $490 to $379, ~30% decline). Google's compression algorithm announcement (reducing LLM key-value cache memory by "at least 6x") created immediate demand destruction. Large AI company reportedly placed "non-binding" orders with both SK Hynix and Samsung simultaneously — a hedging behaviour consistent with demand uncertainty.

**Private credit cracking:** Blackstone's flagship credit fund posted its first monthly loss since 2022 — AI start-ups unable to self-fund beginning to strain private credit markets.
