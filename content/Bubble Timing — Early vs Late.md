# Bubble Timing — Early vs Late

**Source:** Against the Grain | CIO Perspectives (Jan 2026)
**Tags:** #bubbles #timing #risk-management #fiduciary #mental-model

---

## Summary

GQG's position: in a genuine bubble, being early is almost always preferable to being late. The cost of being late — catastrophic drawdown, potential business failure for the manager — asymmetrically exceeds the cost of being early (missed upside, temporary underperformance). This is a foundational mental model underlying their [[GQG Investment Philosophy]].

---

## The Asymmetry Argument

**Cost of being early:**

- Relative underperformance during the bubble's final run
- Client frustration, potential redemptions
- Temporary career risk

**Cost of being late:**

- Catastrophic drawdown when the bubble bursts (often -50% to -90%+ from peak)
- Many firms that held through the dotcom bubble and GFC "disappeared" — absolute business failure
- Investors who held through Micron's 2000 peak waited **25 years** to break even

Given this asymmetry, GQG argues: the rational choice for a fiduciary is to be early.

---

## The Soros Misquote Problem

George Soros's famous line — _"When I see a bubble forming, I rush in to buy"_ — is widely cited to justify chasing bubble stocks. GQG pushes back:

Even Soros's own fund was badly damaged chasing tech stocks in the **final innings** of the 2000 bubble. His legendary protégé **Stanley Druckenmiller resigned** shortly after. The quote is true in the early stages of a bubble; it is fatal advice at the top.

This is a useful inversion: the most dangerous investor behavior in a bubble is not missing the run-up — it is _entering late_ because the narrative has become universally accepted.

---

## "No Bell at the Top"

> _"In my experience, market tops are only obvious in hindsight. No one rings a bell telling you precisely when to exit."_

Management teams are typically _most bullish_ at the peak because earnings are still booming. This creates a paradox: the best fundamental signal (strong reported earnings, bullish management tone) coincides with the worst timing for new investments.

| AI (2025) | Consensus enthusiasm; data center in space narratives | TBD | TBD |

GQG's 2025 AI thesis: they were early. Their assessment: still correct on direction, accepting the timing cost.

## Practical Implication for GQG

Once GQG identifies a bubble:

1. They reduce exposure even before they can prove the timing
2. They accept the relative performance pain
3. They seek to redeploy into overlooked, undervalued sectors ([[Defensive Sectors — 2026 Opportunity]])
4. They wait for "the other side of the bubble" — the unloved assets become the next opportunity

> _"We are thrilled to own the unloved at the valuations offered in the market today."_
