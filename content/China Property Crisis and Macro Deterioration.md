# China Property Crisis and Macro Deterioration

**Source:** Clouded Optimism in China's Markets | GQG Research (Oct 2025)
**Tags:** #china #property #macro #deflation #fiscal #stimulus

---

## Summary

As of October 2025, GQG documented a deepening disconnect between China's stock market rally and its deteriorating economic fundamentals. A prolonged property crisis, deflationary cycle, waning consumer demand, and strained fiscal capacity were all worsening — even as domestic institutional money propped up equity prices. GQG viewed the divergence as unsustainable and corporate earnings as the ultimate arbiter.

---

## The Macro Picture (as of Aug–Sep 2025)

Key indicators all missing expectations simultaneously:

- **Retail sales**: below forecasts
- **Industrial output**: below forecasts
- **Fixed-asset investment (FAI)**: below forecasts; private FAI sharply weaker than state FAI

**Property sector — deepening malaise:**

- New home starts: **-20% YoY** in August
- Property investment: **-6% YoY** in August
- Primary market property sales: **-11% YoY** in August
- Land rights usage transfer revenue: deeply negative
- The property sector's August decline (-13% YoY in fixed-asset investment) was the steepest since the pandemic's onset in 2020

**Government response failing:**

- Interest rate cuts and targeted stimulus have "failed to meaningfully boost private investment or consumer spending"
- Fiscal deficit projected to surge to **9.3% of GDP in 2025** — vs. Beijing's official target of 4%
- The widening fiscal gap is "severely limiting Beijing's ability to deploy aggressive fiscal measures"

**Exports slowing:**

- Export growth slowed from 7.2% (July) to 4.4% (August) — partly due to 50% US tariffs beginning to bite
- Beijing partially offset US export decline with other regions; sustainability uncertain as EU and Southeast Asia also push for protectionism

**Deflationary dynamics:**

- Number of loss-making industrial enterprises rising significantly
- Loss amounts as a percentage of industrial enterprise sales increasing

---

## The Stock Market vs. Reality Disconnect

China and Hong Kong stock markets rallied sharply since late June 2025. GQG's explanation for the divergence:

1. **Domestic institutional money** (record margin trading — outstanding balance of stocks on margin surpassing **\$320 billion**) fuelling price appreciation
2. **Banks not fully recognising non-performing loans** — official earnings data may significantly overstate the true corporate picture
3. **Foreign investors overlooking the data**, captured instead by the narrative of government stimulus and AI momentum

GQG's conclusion: weakening macro fundamentals make share prices "increasingly susceptible to a correction." The test will be corporate earnings, which face growing headwinds.

---

## Connection to Alibaba

GQG used Alibaba as a concrete example of the macro-corporate disconnect. Despite a 70%+ share price rally YTD driven by AI excitement:

- Total revenue grew only 2% YoY (missing estimates)
- Operating profit dropped 3% YoY
- Adjusted non-GAAP net income fell significantly
- Free cash flow: outflow of CNY18.8B vs. inflow of CNY17B a year prior (a CNY~36B swing)
- AI capex surged 220% YoY to CNY39B in one quarter alone
- $53B AI investment plan over 3 years — while China's entire AI application annual recurring revenue is only ~$1.5B (Goldman Sachs, Aug 2025)
