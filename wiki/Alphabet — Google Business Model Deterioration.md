# Alphabet — Google Business Model Deterioration

**Source:** Not Much Alpha Left in This Bet | GQG Research (Mar 2026)
**Tags:** #Alphabet #Google #AI #search #advertising #backward-looking-quality #CapEx

---

## Summary

March 2026 paper — GQG's detailed bear case on Alphabet. The Federer analogy runs throughout: a legendary champion whose style of dominance — nimble, capital-light — is incompatible with the infrastructure-heavy environment they now operate in. GQG held Alphabet as a top holding in early 2024 and exited. Three structural problems: AI is cannibalising the core search business, CapEx has outpaced returns from Google Cloud, and the business has proven far more cyclical than consensus acknowledges.

---

## Problem 1 — AI Is Eating Google's Core Business

Traditional Google search: users click through to websites → multiple ad impressions → revenue. AI-powered search: synthesised direct answers → zero-click behaviour. The shift is already happening:

- By August 2025 (McKinsey): **44% of consumer purchasing journeys were driven by AI-powered search** — surpassing traditional search (31%)
- Analysts estimate **>50% of Google searches now end with zero clicks** to any external website
- Each AI query consumes **~10x more energy** than a traditional precomputed-index search (per Alphabet chairman John Hennessy)

This creates a double-edged sword: AI cannibalises the revenue stream while simultaneously raising the cost to serve each query.

**Advertising market dynamics:** Despite expectations that AI will expand total addressable market, global advertising spend has historically been a fixed share of GDP across every media shift (print → radio → TV → internet → mobile). Digital advertising growth is decelerating. Competition has expanded from the "Big 3" (Google, Meta, Amazon) to include TikTok, Netflix, Walmart, OpenAI, Perplexity, Anthropic, xAI, AppLovin, and The Trade Desk. Consensus expects double-digit advertising growth into perpetuity; GQG views this as unrealistic.

## Problem 2 — Escalating CapEx Without Commensurate Returns

| Metric | Detail |
|---|---|
| Google Cloud CapEx-to-sales ratio | Rose from ~60% in Q1 2023 to **>100% in Q4 2025** — capital spending now exceeds the revenue it generates |
| Uncommenced lease obligations | Jumped from $7.3B (2024) to **>$50B** (end-2025) — binding future commitments to data centres; from ~0 a few years ago |
| GPU depreciation schedule | Alphabet uses **6-year** schedules; GQG's estimate for real economic life: **2–3 years** — same finding as [[Hyperscaler CapEx Circular Accounting]] |
| Reported Google Cloud margins (Q4 2025) | 30.1% on 6-year schedule; **near break-even on a realistic 3-year schedule** |

**New off-balance-sheet liabilities:** Alphabet is quietly backstopping other companies' balance sheets — credit backstops for data centre structures, financial guarantees for energy infrastructure partners. A company that once operated as a capital-light advertising broker is now involved in construction financing. These contingent liabilities require a "high degree of judgment" in accounting.

**Share-based compensation (SBC):** Since 2017, the real economic cost of SBC-related dilution exceeded $140B; total cost to offset SBC dilution and pay related taxes: **>$200B since 2017**. Most adjusted EPS and FCF calculations exclude this entirely. The stock trades at **133x expected next-year FCF** despite FCF being stagnant since 2021 (vs. ~20x pre-COVID).

---

## Problem 3 — Cyclicality Is Underappreciated

Google presented itself as a secular growth business. The 2022 downturn proved otherwise:

- 2022: Alphabet revenue growth fell to 6.7% YoY; net income fell **21%**; the stock plummeted **~40%**
- Management had gone on a spending spree entering the downturn (March 2021: Sundar Pichai pledged $7B in offices/data centres and 10,000 jobs)
- CFO Ruth Porat described "broad-based advertiser strength" in February 2022 — months before the collapse
- Post-mortem (May 2024): "A couple of years ago we accelerated investments..."

**GQG's sensitivity analysis:** If Google Services growth merely matches the overall digital advertising market (~8%), earnings growth would turn **negative** — just as in 2022. The market prices this as a near impossibility.
