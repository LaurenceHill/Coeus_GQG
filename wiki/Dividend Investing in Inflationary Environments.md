# Dividend Investing in Inflationary Environments

**Source:** Quality-First, Dividend Income Approach to Addressing Impacts of Inflation | GQG Research (Sep 2022)
**Tags:** #dividends #inflation #income #fixed-income #duration #quality-growth

---

## Summary

September 2022 paper — written at the peak of the post-COVID inflation cycle (CPI hitting 9.1% in June 2022, Fed Funds rate at 300–325 bps and rising). GQG's argument: in an inflationary, rising-rate environment, income-oriented equities with consistently growing dividends offer better protection than bonds (duration risk) or unprofitable long-duration growth stocks (speculation on distant future cash flows). The paper also articulates GQG's quality-first, forward-looking approach to dividend investing — a framework consistent with how the team manages capital across all cycles.

---

## The Inflation Context (Sep 2022)

- From 1992–2020, US CPI averaged 2.2% annually — secular disinflation from globalisation and technology
- COVID disruption (supply chain + fiscal stimulus + low rates) broke the trend: CPI jumped from 2.6% (Mar 2021) to 9.1% (Jun 2022)
- Additional inflationary pressure: Russia/Ukraine sanctions → higher energy and food; China trade tension → reshoring → higher labour costs
- Historical precedent: 1973–1981 inflation averaged **9.0%** annually, peaking at **13.5% in 1980**
- The Fed Funds rate had already been raised five times in 2022 from 0–25 bps to 300–325 bps; further hikes signalled

---

## Why Bonds Fail in This Environment

Inflation inflicts three simultaneous wounds on fixed income:
1. **Coupon erosion:** Fixed payments lose purchasing power as prices rise
2. **Duration risk:** Rising discount rates compress the present value of future cash flows — longer maturity = larger price decline
3. **Capital loss:** Bond prices fall as yields rise; most corners of the fixed income market posted year-to-date losses through 2022

---

## Why Long-Duration Growth Stocks Fail

"Long-duration stocks" = companies whose cash flows are weighted far into the future (unprofitable growth companies, high-multiple SaaS). These are like very long-duration bonds: rising discount rates compress the present value of distant future profits. In 2022, unprofitable growth stocks experienced some of the worst drawdowns in the equity market — consistent with GQG's capital cycle analysis in [[Capital Cycle Theory — Software and Shale]].

## GQG's Quality-First Dividend Framework

GQG's approach to dividend investing differs from traditional high-yield strategies:

| Traditional Dividend Investing | GQG's Quality-First Approach |
|---|---|
| Screen for current yield | Screen for earnings quality and forward earnings power |
| Static high payer | Consistent dividend *growers* with durable earnings |
| Backward-looking (past dividends) | Forward-looking (projected dividend growth) |
| Risk: dividend cuts when earnings disappoint | Risk mitigation: only hold where earnings durability is high |

The framework is an early statement of what GQG later formalised as [[Forward-Looking Quality vs Backward-Looking Quality]]: seek companies whose best dividend-paying days lie ahead, not those with large current yields that are at risk of being cut.

**Key criteria for GQG dividend holdings:**
- Consistent earnings growth — predictable, non-cyclical revenue
- Strong balance sheet — ability to sustain and grow dividends through cycles
- Pricing power — ability to maintain real dividend value despite inflation
- Moderate payout ratio — room to grow dividends without over-extending
