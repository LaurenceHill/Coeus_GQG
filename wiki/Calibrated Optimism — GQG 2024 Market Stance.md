## Summary

"Calibrated Optimism" was GQG's explicit framing for their mid-2024 market view: broadly positive on fundamentals, overweight tech and healthcare, bullish on India and Brazil, cautious but "less negative at the margin" on China, underweight Japan, and vigilant about the emerging narrative-vs-fundamentals split in AI and software. The Q2 2024 letter also marked the moment GQG began trimming software names while doubling down on NVIDIA and adding consumer staples.

## China: "Less Negative at the Margin" — A Key Nuance

GQG's July 2024 China view is important to track precisely:
- "Some green shoots in narrow areas, such as gaming approvals"
- BUT: *"Every company in China is now functionally a State-Owned Enterprise (SOE), with CCP representation in the boardroom of private sector companies"*
- Crackdown on "conspicuous consumption" (social media removing displays of wealth) — negative for luxury goods, broader consumer sentiment
- Conclusion: *"We do not think things will get materially worse in China from an economic data perspective, it is hard to see things getting meaningfully better either"*

This is measurably less negative than March 2025's *Beyond the Rally* — which made the full bear case — and October 2025's *Clouded Optimism* which documented active deterioration. Consistent trajectory.

## The AI-Software Split (Emerging in Q2 2024)

A key tactical move: GQG observed that across the earnings landscape, many software companies showed misses — suggesting "fundamentals were not keeping up and winners were not nearly as uniform as originally suggested." The market's reaction to software misses in Q1 2024 vs. Q4 2023 was "nearly night and day."

Action taken: Reduced software exposure; re-calibrated into NVIDIA (purchased ~30x forward in 2023, "virtually no multiple expansion" since) and Eli Lilly/GLP-1 names; added consumer staples.

This tactical differentiation — owning NVIDIA while shedding generic AI-adjacent software — foreshadowed GQG's later full AI bubble thesis. The logic was consistent: pay for earnings growth, not narrative.

## GLP-1: Not Just Consumer Risk — Also a Healthcare Opportunity

While GLP-1 drugs were flagged as a risk to alcohol and consumer staples (see [[GLP-1 Impact on Consumer Industries]]), *Context is Key* reveals GQG's other side of that trade: **Eli Lilly** was a core position, representing GLP-1 as a long opportunity:
- Growing evidence of benefits beyond weight loss (e.g., sleep apnea efficacy data from Eli Lilly)
- Consumer staples adapting: Nestlé launched its first new US product suite in ~30 years targeting GLP-1 users
- GQG's view: "Spending will shift to other categories or healthier options over time" — markets and businesses adapt

## "Build the Ark Before the Rain"

One of the clearest articulations of GQG's risk philosophy in this letter:

> *"An ark is built prior to the rain, not during it. Portfolio management is quite similar — we do not need to predict events; we simply need to prepare for them."*

This is the operational expression of [[Bubble Timing — Early vs Late]] and [[GQG Investment Philosophy]] — the position is set in anticipation of disruption, not in reaction to it.

---

## Japan: Structurally Underweight

GQG remained underweight Japan despite TOPIX reaching multi-decade highs in 2024. Reasons:
- Market dominated by cyclicals
- Higher-quality Japanese companies trade at premiums to global peers — poor value proposition
- *"We actually do quite like Japan as tourists, given the abundance of cheap goods for non-Yen holders, we continue to find better opportunities elsewhere"*
