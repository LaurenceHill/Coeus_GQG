# European Opportunity — Banking and Energy

**Source:** A Change of Pace | CIO Perspectives (Apr 2024)
**Tags:** #europe #banks #energy #emerging-from-trough #valuation

---

## Summary

As of Q1 2024, GQG identified Europe as a "less bad is good" opportunity. Energy prices had normalized faster than expected, banking regulatory pressure was easing, and southern European banks and energy companies were offering mid-teens free cash flow yields with low double-digit total shareholder returns — at deep discounts to US equivalents.

---

## Explanation

### The "Less Bad Is Good" Mental Model

GQG used a simple but powerful framing: on a scale of 1-10, moving from 9 to 7 is a disaster; moving from 2 to 4 is wonderful. Europe, having been deeply unloved, was improving from a low base — and markets were pricing in continuation of the worst.

> *"In markets, on a scale of one to ten, if ten is the best and one is the worst, going from nine to seven could be a disaster. On the other hand, going from two to four could be quite wonderful."*

This is a classic Munger-style inversion: ask not what must go right, but what must stop going wrong.

### Energy: Normalization Ahead of Expectations

European energy prices had fallen further and faster than most forecasters predicted. This normalization was broadly positive for the European economy and for energy companies' margins. GQG continued to favor non-US energy companies on both shareholder return and valuation grounds, even as they reduced energy exposure in US portfolios.

### Banking: Credit Growth Returning

The European banking system appeared healthy; regulators had eased. Banks, particularly in **Southern Europe**, were cited as attractive given:
- Policies of increasing returns to shareholders (dividends + buybacks)
- Mid-teens free cash flow yields
- Low double-digit combined shareholder return potential

### What GQG Avoided

The German auto industry was explicitly flagged as a risk — structurally threatened by Chinese EVs, with limited policy recourse given Germany's dependence on Chinese auto sales. A reminder that "Europe improving" ≠ "all of Europe improving."

---

## Valuation Basis (Apr 2024)

Southern European banks and energy companies characterized as:
- Mid-teens FCF yield
- Low double-digit total return (dividends + buybacks)
- High-quality asset bases at attractive multiples
