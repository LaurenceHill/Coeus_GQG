# Multiple Compression Risk

**Sources:** A Spirited Debate | GQG Research (May 2024); A Change of Pace | CIO Perspectives (Apr 2024)
**Tags:** #valuation #de-rating #multiples #risk #spirits #tech

---

## Summary

Multiple compression — the market re-pricing a sector or stock at a lower earnings multiple — is GQG's key valuation risk concept. It occurs when growth expectations fall, when regulatory/structural headwinds emerge, or when macro rates shift. GQG identifies sectors trading at elevated multiples despite deteriorating fundamentals as prime candidates for violent re-ratings.

---

## Explanation

### How Multiple Compression Works

A stock price is roughly: **Price = Earnings × Multiple**

If earnings are flat but the market decides to apply a lower multiple (because it now expects lower future growth, higher risk, or higher interest rates), the stock falls even without an earnings disappointment.

Compression is especially painful when:
1. Earnings are also declining (double-hit)
2. Consensus was extrapolating high growth into perpetuity
3. The catalyst (structural change, regulation) was late to be recognized

### The Tobacco Case Study (GQG Template)

Tobacco became GQG's canonical example of compressed multiples:
- Global cigarette volumes ex-China declined from 2008 onward
- Investors remained comfortable with price/mix sustaining revenues
- **De-rating finally occurred in 2017 — nine years after volume decline began**
- Trigger: increased regulatory scrutiny + accelerating volume declines from reduced-risk products
- The market was slow to re-rate because price/mix masked fundamental weakness for nearly a decade

### Application to Spirits (2024)

GQG in *A Spirited Debate* argued spirits were trading at **near-market multiples** despite:
- Slowing volumes
- Reversing premiumization
- Demographic headwinds ([[Demographic Shifts in Consumer Behavior]])
- GLP-1 risk ([[GLP-1 Impact on Consumer Industries]])
- Early regulatory parallels to tobacco

The market was treating the cyclical hangover as temporary. GQG saw structural deterioration being systematically underpriced.

### Application to Tech (2021 and 2025)

In *A Change of Pace* (Apr 2024), GQG outlined the [[Peak Earnings Peak Multiples Peak Expectations]] framework — where multiple compression is most severe when all three peaks coincide. The same pattern that played out in tech in 2022 was later argued to be re-emerging in AI stocks by 2025 (*Against the Grain*).

SpaceX example (Jan 2026): $800B valuation against $15B revenue while "likely barely profitable" — cited as a textbook case of multiple divorced from fundamental math.

---

## Key Insight

> *"Growth and value are joined at the hip."* — GQG, 2016 whitepaper (cited in *A Spirited Debate*)

Investors who pay high multiples must underwrite proportionately high growth. When growth disappoints, the multiple contracts and the stock falls from both directions simultaneously.
