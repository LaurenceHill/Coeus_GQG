# AI Bubble 2025–2026

**Source:** Against the Grain | CIO Perspectives (Jan 2026)
**Cross-reference:** A Change of Pace | CIO Perspectives (Apr 2024) — earlier, more constructive view
**Tags:** #AI #bubble #dotcom #valuation #narrative-driven #semiconductors

---

## Summary

By January 2026, GQG had concluded — and held firm despite significant relative performance cost — that AI-related stocks had become a speculative bubble disconnected from fundamental earnings math. They drew explicit parallels to the dotcom bubble and the housing bubble, and rotated portfolio exposure away from AI/tech toward defensives.

---

## The Diagnosis

GQG's case for an AI bubble rested on several observations:

**1. Valuations divorced from earnings**
SpaceX cited as emblematic: $800B valuation, $15B revenue, "likely barely profitable." In normal markets, this math doesn't survive. In narrative-driven markets, the story substitutes for the math.

**2. Narrative normalization of impossible expectations**
GQG found investors seriously entertaining the idea of building **data centers in space** to handle AI demand when Earth-based infrastructure becomes constrained. They cite this as a symptom of bubble psychology — the narrative becomes self-reinforcing and validates increasingly absurd premises.

**3. Management teams wildly bullish at the peak**
Historical pattern: leading semiconductor CEOs were "wildly bullish in late 2021, just before the semiconductor sector entered its worst bear market in over a decade." Standing-room-only investor meetings for steel/coal in 2007 and semiconductors in 1999. Bullishness at the top is a warning sign, not a confirmation.

**4. Micron Technology data point**
Micron crossed its year-2000 share price peak "conclusively only in 2025" — a 25-year wait. The 2000 peak was preceded by the narrative of "running out of memory" (due to Japanese foundry fires), followed by a decade-long memory glut. Exact structural parallel to AI infrastructure build-out fears.

**5. The DeepSeek shock (2025)**
When DeepSeek was announced, tech stocks collapsed briefly — a "sneak preview" of how quickly markets can panic when the AI narrative is challenged. Microsoft lost over a third of its value in a single week in 2000 on a comparable narrative shock.

---

## The Cost of Being Early

GQG's AI underweight "cost significant relative performance in 2025." They acknowledge this explicitly. Their defense:
- It is impossible to time a bubble precisely; you are either early or late
- Being late can be fatal (many managers disappeared after the dotcom and GFC)
- George Soros's famous quote ("when I see a bubble forming, I rush in to buy") is often misapplied — Soros's fund was badly hit chasing tech stocks in the final innings of the 2000 bubble; his protégé Stanley Druckenmiller resigned shortly after

> *"In my experience, market tops are only obvious in hindsight. No one rings a bell telling you precisely when to exit."*

---

## ⚠️ Contradiction with Earlier GQG View

*A Change of Pace* (Apr 2024) had GQG **overweight technology and semiconductors** across all four core strategies, bullish on AI-driven capex spending, and explicitly distinguishing 2024's AI rally from the 2021 tech bubble using the [[Peak Earnings Peak Multiples Peak Expectations]] framework.

Between April 2024 and sometime in 2025, GQG changed their view — the bubble diagnosis they said was *absent* in 2024 apparently materialized during 2025. The pivot was costly in relative terms.

This is not a contradiction so much as an evolution — they were adaptive, as their philosophy dictates. But it illustrates the difficulty: even a fundamentals-first manager had the bubble thesis arrive *during* the bubble, not before it.
