## Summary

April 2025 paper on what GQG describes as an underappreciated "tectonic shift" in the global monetary order. The US dollar has been the world's primary reserve currency for over 100 years — historically the approximate lifespan of any reserve currency. Against a backdrop of unprecedented US debt accumulation, unorthodox monetary policy, global conflicts, and the rise of digital currencies, GQG argues that investors must consider the possibility of a meaningful monetary regime change — even if the timing is unknowable. The monetary system is the "base layer" of all financial transactions; getting it wrong breaks asset prices, earnings, and cash flow valuations.

## What Makes This Moment Different

Every prior major reserve currency operated with *some* commodity backing at some level. The critical break:

- **Bretton-Woods (post-WW2):** Gold-convertibility standard for governments ($35/oz). Countries could exchange dollars for gold; individuals could not.
- **Nixon Shock (August 1971):** The gold window closed. The US dollar became a pure fiat currency — **backed only by the economic importance of the US and trust in its institutions.**
- Since then: the US dollar is the first global reserve currency to operate in a **fully fiat, non-commodity-backed system for multiple decades.** This is historically unprecedented.

Historical pattern preceding monetary regime shifts: **debt accumulation + currency debasement + war** — all three of which are present in 2025.

Compound annual growth rates from Q4 1971 to Q1 2025:

| Metric | Q4 1971 | Q1 2025 | CAGR |
|---|---|---|---|
| US Federal Debt | $424B | $35,465B | **8.7%** |
| Gold | $35/oz | $2,820/oz | **8.5%** |
| S&P 500 (price only) | 85 | 5,882 | **8.2%** |
| US Money Supply M2 | $698B | $21,561B | **6.6%** |

Key insight: **Federal debt has grown faster than any other asset class since Nixon closed the gold window.** Gold has almost perfectly tracked federal debt accumulation as a store of value.

## Alternatives to the Dollar — Gold and Bitcoin

GQG sees two categories of alternatives emerging:

### Gold (Old Store of Value)
- Meets Aristotle's criteria for sound money: durable, portable, divisible, intrinsically valuable, and scarce
- Cannot be "conjured up out of thin air" — in contrast to fiat currencies
- CAGR of 8.5% since 1971 almost exactly matches US debt growth — functioning as a hedge against debasement

### Bitcoin (New Store of Value)
- Described by GQG as meeting many of Aristotle's money criteria, similar to gold
- Rise rooted in its proposition as a fully decentralized, peer-to-peer digital cash system
- GQG does not advocate for Bitcoin as an investment — presents it as a symptom of monetary system doubt

### Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) — The Negative Case
- CBDCs = **potentially even more inflationary** than the current system
- No supply limits + potential for expiry dates = loss of money's core function as a store of value
- Represents a further weakening of the monetary system's trustworthiness, not a solution

## GQG's Investment Framework Implications

GQG explicitly frames this as a **base-layer risk** — not a specific near-term call, but a scenario that cannot be ignored when pricing assets:

> *"Because the monetary system is the base layer of all financial transactions, impacting purchasing power but also the value of earnings and cash flows, we do not believe these monetary movements can be ignored."*

Practical implications GQG draws:
1. Asset price uncertainty increases when the monetary measuring stick itself is unstable
2. Companies with real assets, pricing power, and genuine earnings are more robust than those whose valuations depend on stable nominal discount rates
3. Gold's consistent tracking of debt accumulation over 50+ years suggests it remains a relevant inflation and debasement hedge
