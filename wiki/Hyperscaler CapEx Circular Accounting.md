# Hyperscaler CapEx Circular Accounting

**Source:** Dotcom on Steroids: Part III | GQG Research (Dec 2025)
**Tags:** #accounting #AI #CapEx #S&P500 #earnings #semiconductors #cyclicality

---

## Summary

GQG's December 2025 paper quantified precisely how hyperscaler AI capital expenditure is inflating S&P 500 reported earnings through a cascading accounting effect — and why the reversal, when it comes, will be severe. The mechanics exploit the mismatch between revenue recognition (immediate) and depreciation (spread over years), creating artificial profits that are front-loaded and will reverse when CapEx growth slows.

---

## The Accounting Mechanics

**The cascade:**
When Company A (hyperscaler) spends $100 on CapEx:
- A records only incremental depreciation on its income statement (small, spread over 5-6 years under current assumptions)
- Company B (Tier-1 supplier: NVIDIA, Broadcom, Arista) records $100 as **immediate revenue**, earning ~40% operating margins → **~$40 in operating income**
- Company B's own CapEx from that revenue flows to Company C (Tier-2: power supply, cooling, data centre construction) → **~$25 in further operating income** (at ~25% margins, on 50% revenue flow-through)

**GQG's 2025 estimate:**
| Category | Amount |
|---|---|
| Hyperscaler CapEx (est. 2025) | $395B |
| AI start-up investment (est. 2025) | $145B |
| Total AI-related spend driving demand | $540B |
| Tier-1 operating income contribution | ~$151B |
| Tier-2 operating income contribution | ~$47B |
| **Total operating income distortion** | **~$198B** |
| S&P 500 operating income growth (est. 2025) | ~$586B |
| **GQG's estimated AI CapEx contribution** | **~34%** |

One third of all S&P 500 earnings growth in 2025 was, in GQG's estimate, an artefact of circular AI spending — not organic business growth.

## The Depreciation Assumption Problem

The AI accounting inflation is further amplified by aggressive depreciation assumptions. The market is baking in **5-6 year useful lives** for AI hardware (servers, chips). GQG's view: given the pace of GPU and model improvement, the actual useful life is closer to **2-3 years**.

If GQG is right:
- Annual depreciation charges are significantly understated today
- When hardware is written down faster (or when the next generation renders current hardware obsolete quickly), a large depreciation catch-up hits earnings all at once
- This acts as a multiplier on the CapEx deceleration effect — the revenue miss *and* the depreciation reset hit simultaneously

## The Semiconductor Cyclicality Problem

An additional structural vulnerability: semiconductors now represent **~15% of the S&P 500** — up from a **~4% median weight** over the preceding decade.

Why this matters:
- Semiconductors are among the most cyclical industries in existence
- In the prior cycle (2021-2022), semiconductor *stock prices* peaked ~6 months before *earnings expectations* began to fall — by the time consensus estimates adjusted, prices had already collapsed by more than a third
- Global semiconductor sales are currently **50%+ above their long-term trend** — a level historically associated with sharp mean-reversion
- Even the best semiconductor executives could not give advance warning before the previous decline — the industry is structurally opaque at turning points

> *"I would expect that we will see a supply constrained environment for the vast majority of next year."* — CEO comment near the 2021 semiconductor peak (subsequently wrong)

With 15% of the index now exposed to this cyclicality, an S&P 500 earnings correction from semiconductor normalisation alone could be material.

## The VC Amplifier

Venture capital's $145B in AI start-up investment (2025 estimate) acts as a second-order demand driver — VC-funded start-ups buying GPU compute generate revenue for the same Tier-1 suppliers (NVIDIA, etc.) that hyperscalers fund. When VC sentiment turns (or when AI start-ups burn through their capital without finding sustainable revenue), this demand collapses simultaneously with hyperscaler CapEx deceleration.

**Oracle warning sign (Apr 2026):** Oracle's credit default swap spread surpassed its GFC peak — the market beginning to price credit risk into the "poster child for the insatiable debt-fuelled growth binge." See [[Dire Straits — Energy Shock and Geopolitical Risk 2026]].

## The OECD Data Point

The OECD published reports indicating that absent large-scale AI-related spending, real GDP growth in the first half of 2025 would have been **negative**. This is GQG's most striking data point: AI CapEx has become so large that it is now a measurable support for the entire economy. When it decelerates, the macro impact will extend well beyond tech stock prices.
