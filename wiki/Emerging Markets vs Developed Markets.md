# Emerging Markets vs Developed Markets

**Sources:** Awakening the Sleeping Giant (Nov 2023); Beyond the Rally (Mar 2025); A Change of Pace (Apr 2024)
**Tags:** #emerging-markets #developed-markets #allocation #geopolitics #india #brazil #china

## Summary

GQG's EM framework is highly selective and reform-aware rather than index-driven. As of 2024-2025, they are bullish on Brazil, India, and select EM ex-China, while viewing China as structurally uninvestable for long-term investors and cautious on developed markets where AI valuations had become extreme.

---

## GQG's EM Allocation Principles

1. **Economic growth ≠ equity returns**: China's experience proves that extraordinary GDP growth can coexist with near-zero shareholder returns when governance is poor (see [[SOE Reform and Corporate Governance]])
2. **Reform trajectory matters more than current quality**: Brazil was attractive precisely because its reform programme was underappreciated
3. **Valuation discipline always applies**: EM attractiveness is always price-conditional
4. **Geopolitical risk is investable, but not ignorable**: China's Cold War-type relationship with the West is a structural discount that cannot be arbitraged away

## Country-Level Views (as of mid-2024 to early-2025)

| Country/Region | GQG Stance | Key Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Brazil | Bullish | Underappreciated 2016-2022 reforms; pragmatic policy; attractive valuations |
| India | Bullish | Structural growth story; large domestic market; democratic governance |
| China | Avoid (long-term) | 30 years zero returns; SOE governance; geopolitical escalation; demographic headwinds |
| Europe (Southern) | Selectively bullish | Banks + energy; "less bad is good"; improving from low base |
| US (tech) | Cautious/turned cautious | AI bubble concerns emerged through 2025 |
| US (defensives) | Bullish by Jan 2026 | Extreme relative cheapness; quality compounders overlooked |
